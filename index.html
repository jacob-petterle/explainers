How a Kubernetes YAML file becomes a hardware-enforced private network — multi-tenancy for bare-metal clouds, where you can't trust a single byte of software on the tenant's server.

K8s & the Packet

Kubernetes never touches your traffic. It programs the machines that do. Once you see that, "does bare metal route through Kubernetes?" answers itself.

RoCE & the Switch

Bare Metal & Provisioning

4 entries
Out-of-band Mgmt→

BMC & Redfish

The tiny always-on computer inside every server — and the modern REST API used to command entire fleets of them.
Provisioning→

Metal³

"Metal Kubed." Physical servers become Kubernetes resources: kubectl apply a YAML file, and a real machine in a real rack powers on, wipes itself, and installs your image.
Fleet Ops→

Fleet Management

Redfish gives every server the same API. These are the tools that stand on top of it — and the honest answer to "can I see my whole fleet in one place?"
Source of Truth→

NetBox

The database of what your infrastructure is supposed to look like. It never touches a machine — and that's exactly why everything else depends on it.
03

Observability

2 entries
Metrics→

Prometheus

The metrics engine of modern infrastructure: it goes out, asks every system "how are you?" on a schedule, remembers the answers as time series, and raises the alarm when they go wrong.
Dashboards→

Grafana

The open-source glass over your infrastructure: it doesn't store your data — it queries every system where it lives and composes the answers into dashboards, alerts, and custom UIs.

Kafka + Iceberg

Hot systems keep weeks; the lakehouse keeps years. Kafka is the conveyor belt that never drops anything; Iceberg turns plain files in object storage into real, queryable tables.

Temporal

Write multi-step business logic as ordinary code, and the platform guarantees it runs to completion — through crashes, deploys, and outages — whether it takes seconds or months.
